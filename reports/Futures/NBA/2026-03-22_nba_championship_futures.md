## Methodology

1. **Fetched** NBA Championship winner outright odds from 4 US sportsbooks
2. **De-vigged** by normalizing implied probabilities per book (N-way de-vig across 26 teams)
3. **Consensus** = median de-vigged championship probability across all books
4. **Converted** championship probability to conference winner probability:
   - `P(conference winner) = min(2 * P(champion), 0.95)`
   - Rationale: to win the title, you must first win your conference. If there were only 2 equally likely finalists, P(conf) = 2 * P(champ). In practice, the favorite's conference probability is slightly less than 2x due to path difficulty, but this is a reasonable approximation.
5. **Compared** to Kalshi conference winner ask prices

## Caveats

- **Conference-to-championship conversion is approximate.** The 2x multiplier assumes roughly equal Finals matchup odds. In reality, OKC might face a weak East finalist, making their path easier than 2x suggests (i.e., our model may *underestimate* their conference probability).
- **4 books** is decent but not as robust as 8-10. More books would tighten the consensus.
- **Playoffs start mid-April.** These positions settle within ~2 months, much shorter lockup than MLB futures.
- **The OKC edge is enormous** (+26.3%) -- this level of mispricing is unusual and worth scrutiny. Verify by checking other sources (ESPN BPI, FiveThirtyEight, Vegas consensus).
