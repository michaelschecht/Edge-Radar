# Edge-Radar Trade History

Two log files maintained by the executor and settler. Both are gitignored.

| File | Writer | Purpose |
|------|--------|---------|
| `kalshi_trades.json` | `scripts/kalshi/kalshi_executor.py` | Pre-execution context: composite score, risk approval, edge source, bankroll %, fill status. One record per order placement. |
| `kalshi_settlements.json` | `scripts/kalshi/kalshi_settler.py` | Settled-trade ledger with realized P&L, revenue, fees, ROI. One record per settled trade. Carries the full trade-side context as of R5 (2026-04-27). |

The two files join on `trade_id` (UUID generated by the executor at order time and copied through to the settlement record).

## Reconciliation health

Run `python scripts/kalshi/risk_check.py --report reconciliation` to see:

- Trade-log / settlement counts and `trade_id` overlap
- Orphaned-settlement window
- R5-added field coverage (per field, % of settlements where it's populated)

## Pre-R5 historical cohort

Settlements written before 2026-04-27 carry only the legacy schema:

```
trade_id, ticker, side, result, won, contracts, cost, revenue, fees,
net_pnl, roi, edge_estimated, fair_value, market_price_at_entry,
confidence, settled_at
```

R5 (2026-04-27) extended the schema to also carry `order_id`, `title`, `category`, `edge_source`, `closing_price`, `clv`, `composite_score`, `risk_approval`, `bankroll_pct`, `unit_size`, and `fill_status`.

These pre-R5 records will show as `null` for the R5-added fields in the reconciliation report. **No backfill is planned** — the missing fields don't exist anywhere on disk and synthesizing them would be fabricating data. Calibration analytics (`scripts/kalshi/model_calibration.py`) read settlement-side fields and degrade gracefully on absent ones; what's lost on this cohort is the ability to slice by composite-score bucket, risk-approval flag, or bankroll allocation.

The trade log itself was reset at some point before 2026-04-27, so 100% of pre-R5 settlements are also orphans (no matching `kalshi_trades.json` entry to join). This is expected and is what motivated R5 in the first place.
